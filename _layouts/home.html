About Me

I’m a third-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at UMass Amherst, working at the intersection of decision-making under uncertainty an Econ\CS. I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Hedyeh Beyhaghi and co-advised by Prof. Mohammad Hajiesmaili. I collaborate closely with the Solar Lab and was previously part of the FED Lab, under the supervision of Yair Zick. Additionally, I had the privilege of working under the tutelage of Prof. Mohammad Ghodsi during my academic journey.

My research focuses on applying online learning in computatuinal economics, particularly in algorithmic game theory, matching markets, and resource allocation. I'm also deeply interested in statistical learning theory and multi-armed bandits, aiming to design efficient algorithms that balance exploration and exploitation in uncertain environments.
